Project concept note · v1.0 · for field & technical partners

Rapid-deployment, climate-adapted classrooms for children in South Sudan.

Across South Sudan, climate disasters and protracted conflict have left hundreds of thousands of children without safe, usable learning spaces. Cuick School proposes a standardized modular classroom unit — transported and assembled quickly in low-resource environments, engineered for extreme heat, and paired with established emergency-education curricula.

The problem

An urgent gap in safe learning spaces.

200K+
children affected by recent climate disasters in the region
40°C+
routine ambient temperatures that make unventilated structures unsafe for learning
Weeks
to months lost to conventional school construction in displacement settings

Conventional construction is too slow, and tented spaces too hot, for the conditions children are learning in. Cuick School sits between the two: a unit that deploys at the speed of an emergency and holds up to the climate of one.

Our solution

A modular classroom unit, built on four principles.

The unit is designed to distinguish itself from both permanent construction and tented temporary spaces — fast to stand up, safe in the heat, and ready to teach from day one.

01

Rapid deployment

Pre-fabricated, flat-packable components assembled on-site by a small trained team with minimal equipment.

02

Climate adaptation

Passive ventilation, a reflective and insulated envelope, and shading engineered to keep interior temperatures within a safe learning range.

03

Curriculum-ready

Each unit pairs with proven emergency-education kits and foundational literacy and numeracy programs, rather than reinventing pedagogy.

04

Low-cost & repeatable

A standardized bill of materials enabling predictable per-unit cost and scaling across sites.

Standards & safeguarding

Built to recognized humanitarian standards.

We are developing the model against the frameworks the sector already uses — and we want partner specialists to check our work before anything is deployed.

Education in emergencies
INEE Minimum Standards
The unit and its programming are developed in reference to the INEE Minimum Standards for Education in Emergencies.
Shelter & WASH
Sphere Standards
Shelter and water, sanitation, and hygiene considerations are guided by the Sphere Standards.

The project operates under a written child safeguarding commitment. We actively seek partner review of our climate and safeguarding approach to ensure full compliance before any deployment — not after.
