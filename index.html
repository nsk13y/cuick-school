Project concept · v1.2 · for field & technical partners

Rapid-deployment, climate-adapted classrooms for children in South Sudan.

Across South Sudan, climate disasters and protracted conflict have left hundreds of thousands of children without safe, usable learning spaces. Cuick School proposes a standardized modular classroom unit — a retrofitted shipping container transported and assembled quickly in low-resource environments, engineered for extreme heat, and paired with established emergency-education curricula.

Our purpose

"A world where every child displaced by conflict or climate has immediate access to a safe, well-resourced learning environment."

Brighter Boxes × Cuick School · student-founded · building toward it one container at a time

The problem

Right now in South Sudan, a child is more likely to be out of school than in one.

2.8M+ children out of school — over 70% of the school-age population, most of them girls (UNICEF). And in 40°C+ heat, even the classrooms that exist are closing.

Project concept note

Why shipping containers?

R1

A global surplus

Used ISO containers are abundant and inexpensive to source in port economies like South Korea — turning a waste stream into school infrastructure.

R2

Transport-native

A container moves through the world's existing shipping network as standard cargo. No special freight, no custom handling: the school is its own crate.

R3

Durable & securable

A weathertight steel shell withstands rough handling and harsh climates — and locks, protecting solar equipment and supplies between school days.

Built to recognized humanitarian standards.

The project operates under a written child safeguarding commitment. We develop the model against the frameworks the sector already uses — and we actively seek partner review of our climate and safeguarding approach to ensure full compliance before any deployment, not after.

INEE Minimum Standards

The long-term vision

From one unit to a campus.

The first container is proof, not the finish line. Cuick School is designed from day one as a system that scales — structurally, energetically, and locally.

V1

Modular, Lego-style scaling

The unit is the building block, not the building. Standardized units combine into multi-classroom clusters and, eventually, full campuses — with layouts adapted to each region's climate, terrain, and community needs.

V2

Smart energy systems Roadmap

Solar generation paired with battery storage and simple load management, so a unit banks power where electricity access is inconsistent — keeping lights, fans, and lesson screens running through the school day.

V3

Locally run, by design

Deployment ends with handover, not dependence. Local teams assemble and maintain units, community teachers deliver the lessons, and partner NGOs anchor operations on the ground.

Our solution

The unit, the program, and the journey between.

A modular classroom unit built on four principles — fast to stand up, safe in the heat, ready to teach from day one, and cheap enough to repeat.

01

Rapid deployment

Pre-fabricated, flat-packable components assembled on-site by a small trained team with minimal equipment — days of assembly instead of months of construction.

02

Climate adaptation

Passive ventilation, a reflective and insulated envelope, and shading engineered to keep interior temperatures within a safe learning range — in the same 40°C+ conditions that close iron-sheet schools.

03

Curriculum-ready

Each unit pairs with proven emergency-education (EiE) kits and foundational literacy and numeracy programs, rather than reinventing pedagogy — managed by our Textbook Team below.

04

Low-cost & repeatable

A standardized bill of materials enabling predictable per-unit cost and scaling across sites — the same spec, every time, anywhere.

From Songdo to the school site.

The model's quiet advantage: the classroom ships as its own crate. Brighter Boxes sources a surplus shipping container in Korea, retrofits it to the Cuick spec, packs it with its own education payload, and sends it through the same global logistics network as any commercial cargo. The Textbook Team — remote curriculum management.

A container is only a classroom if there's something worth teaching inside it. Our education team (Pillar A) builds and manages that layer remotely from Songdo — designed for settings with low literacy, no internet, and limited electricity.

A1

Adapt, don't reinvent

We package proven emergency-education materials and foundational literacy & numeracy programs — adapted for low-resource, offline classrooms — rather than writing pedagogy from scratch.

A2

Offline by design

Printed subject packets in math, reading, health, and art, plus short video micro-lessons for TV/USB playback. No internet connection required, ever.

A3

School-in-a-Box

Every unit ships with a complete teacher kit — lesson guides, materials, and supplies — so a community teacher can run a functional classroom from day one.

A4

Managed remotely, refreshed physically

Curriculum is versioned in Songdo and updated through USB refresh packs and reprints delivered on partner visits — with a feedback loop from local teachers shaping every revision.
